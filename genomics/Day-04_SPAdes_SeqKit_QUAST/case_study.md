# Case Study

# Evaluating Genome Assembly Quality

## Background

Genome assembly combines millions of sequencing reads into longer DNA sequences.

The quality of assembly determines the success of downstream analyses.

---

## Problem

Poor-quality sequencing reads may produce:

- Fragmented assemblies
- Low N50
- Large number of contigs
- Missing genomic regions

---

## Solution

Use SPAdes for assembly and QUAST to evaluate assembly quality.

SeqKit provides additional sequence statistics for validation.

---

## Real-world Example

Bacterial genome sequencing projects routinely evaluate assemblies before genome annotation.

Assembly statistics help researchers decide whether sequencing data are suitable for publication and further analysis.

---

## Conclusion

Genome assembly quality assessment is essential for producing reliable genomic analyses.
