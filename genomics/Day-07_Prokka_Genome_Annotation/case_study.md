# Case Study

# Functional Annotation of a Bacterial Genome

## Background

A bacterial genome was assembled successfully, but the biological functions of the predicted genes were unknown.

---

## Problem

Without functional annotation, researchers cannot identify proteins, metabolic pathways, or important genes.

---

## Solution

The assembled genome was annotated using Prokka.

Predicted genes were assigned functional information and exported in multiple annotation formats.

---

## Real-world Example

Prokka is widely used for annotating newly sequenced bacterial genomes before comparative genomics and metabolic pathway analysis.

---

## Conclusion

Genome annotation transforms raw DNA sequences into biologically meaningful information and supports downstream functional analyses.
