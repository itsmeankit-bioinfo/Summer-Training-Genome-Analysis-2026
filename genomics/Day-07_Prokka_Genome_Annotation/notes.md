# Notes

# What is Genome Annotation?

Genome annotation is the process of identifying genomic features and assigning biological functions to DNA sequences.

---

# Types of Genome Annotation

## Structural Annotation

Identifies:

- Protein-coding genes
- tRNA genes
- rRNA genes
- Regulatory regions

---

## Functional Annotation

Assigns biological functions to predicted genes using sequence similarity and databases.

---

# What is Prokka?

Prokka is an automated genome annotation pipeline designed for bacterial and archaeal genomes.

It integrates multiple bioinformatics tools to identify genes and assign functional annotations.

---

# Tools Used by Prokka

- Prodigal – Gene prediction
- Barrnap – rRNA prediction
- Aragorn – tRNA prediction
- BLAST+ – Sequence similarity search
- HMMER – Protein family search

---

# Prokka Output Files

## GFF

Genome annotation file.

---

## GBK

GenBank formatted annotation.

---

## FAA

Predicted protein sequences.

---

## FNA

Predicted nucleotide coding sequences.

---

## FFN

Gene nucleotide sequences.

---

## SQN

Submission-ready annotation file.

---

## TSV

Annotation summary.

# Why Genome Annotation is Important

Genome annotation allows researchers to:

- Identify genes
- Predict proteins
- Study metabolic pathways
- Compare genomes
- Understand organism biology

# Key Takeaways
