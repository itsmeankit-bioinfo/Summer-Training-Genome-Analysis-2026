# Case Study

# Identifying Antibiotic Resistance in a Bacterial Genome

## Background

A bacterial isolate was sequenced and annotated.

Researchers wanted to determine whether it carried genes associated with antibiotic resistance.

---

## Problem

Genome annotation identified genes, but their biological functions and resistance mechanisms were unknown.

---

## Solution

Protein sequences were submitted to BlastKOALA for functional annotation and analyzed using an AMR database to identify resistance genes.

The combined results revealed metabolic pathways and potential antimicrobial resistance mechanisms.

---

## Real-world Example

Hospitals and public health laboratories use functional annotation and AMR analysis to monitor drug-resistant pathogens and guide infection control strategies.

---

## Conclusion

Functional annotation combined with AMR analysis provides important biological insights beyond genome assembly and supports research, diagnostics, and public health.
