# Notes

# What is Functional Annotation?

Functional annotation is the process of assigning biological functions to predicted genes and proteins.

It helps researchers understand the biological role of genes within an organism.

---

# Structural vs Functional Annotation

| Structural Annotation | Functional Annotation |
|----------------------|-----------------------|
| Identifies genes | Assigns gene function |
| Predicts ORFs | Predicts biological roles |
| Genome coordinates | Metabolic pathways |

---

# What is BlastKOALA?

BlastKOALA is an online KEGG tool that assigns KEGG Orthology (KO) identifiers to protein sequences.

It compares predicted proteins against the KEGG GENES database.

---

# KEGG Orthology (KO)

A KO identifier represents a functional group of homologous genes.

Genes with the same KO generally perform similar biological functions.

---

# KEGG Pathways

KEGG organizes genes into biological pathways such as:

- Glycolysis
- TCA Cycle
- DNA Replication
- Amino Acid Metabolism
- Lipid Metabolism
- Cell Signaling

---

# Why Functional Annotation is Important

Functional annotation allows researchers to:

- Predict protein function
- Reconstruct metabolic pathways
- Study disease mechanisms
- Compare organisms
- Understand microbial physiology

---

# What is Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)?

Antimicrobial Resistance is the ability of microorganisms to survive exposure to antimicrobial drugs.

Resistance develops through genetic mutations or acquisition of resistance genes.

---

# AMR Genes

Examples include genes involved in:

- Beta-lactam resistance
- Aminoglycoside resistance
- Tetracycline resistance
- Macrolide resistance
- Fluoroquinolone resistance

---

# Importance of AMR Analysis

AMR analysis helps:

- Detect resistant pathogens
- Guide antibiotic therapy
- Monitor resistance trends
- Support public health surveillance
