# Case Study

# Metagenome Assembly of a Soil Sample

## Background

A soil sample contains DNA from thousands of microorganisms.

---

## Problem

Short sequencing reads cannot directly reveal complete genomes.

---

## Solution

Metagenomic reads were assembled using SPAdes and MEGAHIT.

Bowtie2 was then used to align sequencing reads back to the assembled contigs.

---

## Outcome

The assembled contigs and alignment results were used for downstream analyses such as genome binning and quality assessment.

---

## Conclusion

Metagenome assembly and sequence alignment form the foundation of genome-resolved metagenomics.
