# Case Study

# Assembly of a Soil Metagenome

## Background

Researchers sequenced DNA extracted from agricultural soil to study microbial diversity.

---

## Problem

The sequencing data contained DNA from thousands of microorganisms.

Direct biological interpretation was impossible using short sequencing reads.

---

## Solution

MEGAHIT assembled the sequencing reads into longer contigs.

These contigs were later used for genome binning and functional annotation.

---

## Real-world Example

Environmental microbiologists routinely assemble metagenomic datasets to discover novel microorganisms and metabolic pathways involved in nutrient cycling.

---

## Conclusion

Metagenome assembly transforms millions of short sequencing reads into biologically meaningful DNA sequences for downstream analyses.
