# Case Study

# Reconstructing Microbial Genomes from a Marine Metagenome

## Background

Researchers collected seawater samples to investigate microbial diversity.

---

## Problem

The assembled metagenome contained thousands of contigs from many different microorganisms.

Individual genomes could not be identified directly.

---

## Solution

SeqKit was used to inspect and summarize the assembled sequences.

MetaBAT2 grouped contigs into genome bins using sequence composition and coverage information.

---

## Outcome

Genome bins (MAGs) representing individual microbial genomes were reconstructed for downstream taxonomic and functional analyses.

---

## Conclusion

Genome binning is an essential step in transforming metagenomic assemblies into biologically meaningful genomes.
