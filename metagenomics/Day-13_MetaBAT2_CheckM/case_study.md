# Case Study

# Reconstructing High-quality MAGs from a Wastewater Sample

## Background

Researchers collected wastewater samples to investigate microbial diversity and identify antibiotic-resistant microorganisms.

---

## Problem

The metagenome assembly contained mixed contigs from many different microorganisms.

Individual genomes could not be analyzed directly.

---

## Solution

MetaBAT2 grouped contigs into genome bins using sequence composition and coverage information.

CheckM evaluated the completeness and contamination of each reconstructed genome.

---

## Outcome

High-quality MAGs were identified and selected for downstream taxonomic and functional analyses.

---

## Conclusion

Genome binning and MAG quality assessment are essential steps in genome-resolved metagenomics.
