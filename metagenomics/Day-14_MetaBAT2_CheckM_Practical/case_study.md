# Case Study

# Genome Binning of an Environmental Metagenome

## Background

Researchers analyzed a metagenomic dataset from an environmental sample to reconstruct microbial genomes.

---

## Problem

The assembled contigs originated from many microorganisms and required separation into individual genomes.

---

## Solution

MetaBAT2 grouped contigs into genome bins using coverage information and sequence composition.

CheckM evaluated each genome bin to identify high-quality MAGs.

---

## Outcome

Several high-quality genome bins were obtained and selected for downstream taxonomic and functional analyses.

---

## Conclusion

Genome binning followed by quality assessment enables the reconstruction of reliable microbial genomes from complex metagenomic datasets.
